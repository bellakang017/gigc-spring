Sharpen Your Thinking

Top firms like McKinsey now test how you think through problems — not what you already know.
Here are three tools they look for. Let's practice them right now.

🔍

Ask

A vague question gets a vague answer. Sharpen the question first.

🧩

Structure

Break a big messy problem into clean pieces. Think out loud, not in circles.

✏️

Refine

Your first answer is a draft. Look for what's missing and make it better.

🔍 Ask — Make this question sharper

Notice the difference: "I can't do this" shuts the door. "How can I learn to do this?" opens it. The same applies to interview questions — a sharper question gets you a sharper answer.
"I'm not good at interviews. How do I fix that?"

🧩 Structure — Sort this into clean buckets

What are "clean buckets"?

In consulting, they call this MECE (say "me-see"). It just means: your categories shouldn't overlap, and together they should cover everything. That's it.

Overlapping (not MECE)"Skills" and "Hard skills" — one lives inside the other.
Clean (MECE)"Mindset," "Preparation," "Communication" — each is separate, together they cover a lot.
"What holds people back from showing their best selves in interviews?"

✏️ Refine — What's missing from this advice?

This advice sounds safe — but it's all about avoiding mistakes instead of actually growing. Read it and think: what's missing?
"To do well in interviews, memorize your answers, avoid talking about weaknesses, and stick to what you already know."
