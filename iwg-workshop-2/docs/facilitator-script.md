# 🐝 Interview Instinct — Facilitator Script

**IWG Workshop #2 · February 24, 2026 · 3:00–4:00 PM · FAC 2.134**
**Facilitator: Bella Kang · Graduate Global Impact Consultant**

## Pre-Workshop (2:45–3:00)

- [ ] Print handouts (1 per person)
- [ ] Test projector → open `interview-instinct.html` fullscreen
- [ ] Set chairs in pairs (partner work later)
- [ ] Cue slide to hero screen, bee floating
- [ ] Have red envelopes ready (STORY prompt inside)
- [ ] Background music low (optional, warm tone)

## 1 · Welcome & Ground (3:00–3:08) — 8 min

### Open (2 min)
- "Welcome to Workshop #2 — Interview Instinct"
- Name, role, one-liner: "Today we find YOUR natural interview style"
- Jane Goodall quote on screen → read aloud
- Frame: "Interviews aren't about performing. They're about showing up as YOU."

### Name + Warm-Up (3 min)
- Everyone writes name on handout
- Quick icebreaker: "Turn to your neighbor — what animal would you be in a job interview? No wrong answers."
- 60 seconds each, then 2–3 share with group

### Set Expectations (3 min)
- "Three things we'll do today:"
  - Find your instinct (what you naturally bring)
  - Build a HIVE story (structured storytelling)
  - Sharpen your thinking (how top firms actually evaluate you)
- "This is practice, not performance. Messy is fine."

---

## 2 · Animal Quiz — Find Your Instinct (3:08–3:20) — 12 min

### Introduce the Grid (2 min)
- Show animal grid on screen (scroll or click to section)
- "8 animals, 8 interview superpowers"
- "There's no best animal. Each one is a real strength."
- Read through quickly: Lamb = trust, Cat = reads the room, Pup = solver...

### Individual Selection (3 min)
- "Circle ONE on your handout. Go with gut — don't overthink."
- "Write your animal, superpower, and story prompt in the 'My Instinct' box"
- Walk around, check in quietly

### Red Envelope Moment (4 min)
- "Now open your red envelope"
- Inside: STORY prompt card that connects to their animal type
- "This is your interview story seed — a prompt designed for YOUR instinct"
- 2 min silent reflection: jot initial thoughts on handout margin

### Quick Share (3 min)
- "Raise your hand if you're a Lamb... Cat... Pup..."
- Acknowledge diversity: "See? We need all of these in a team."
- 2–3 volunteers: "Tell us your animal and why in one sentence"

---

## 3 · Build Your HIVE (3:20–3:40) — 20 min

### Teach HIVE (4 min)
- Show HIVE section on screen
- "STAR is fine. But HIVE is designed for YOU — international students telling cross-cultural stories."
- Walk through each letter:
  - **H** — Habitat: "Don't just say 'at my job.' Paint the scene. Country, team size, stakes."
  - **I** — Initiative: "Why YOU? What made you step up, not someone else?"
  - **V** — Venture: "Specific actions. Not 'I helped.' What exactly did you DO?"
  - **E** — Effect: "Numbers. Percentages. Before/after. Make it real."
- Model a quick example (30 sec, your own story or a generic one)

### Individual Writing (6 min)
- "Use your animal's story prompt OR your red envelope prompt"
- "Fill in all four HIVE boxes on your handout"
- "Don't worry about perfect sentences — bullet points are fine"
- Walk around, nudge stuck people: "Start with H — where were you?"

### Partner Practice — 60 Seconds (8 min)
- "Find a partner. Person A tells their HIVE story. 60 seconds."
- Start timer on screen (or use handout's timer if projecting HTML)
- After 60s: "Partner B — give one strength and one suggestion. 30 seconds."
- Switch roles → repeat
- "Write partner feedback on your handout"

### Debrief (2 min)
- "What was harder — telling the story or keeping it to 60 seconds?"
- "That's normal. The structure helps you edit, not memorize."
- Key insight: "Your instinct animal tells you WHERE your story shines. HIVE tells you HOW to tell it."

---

## 4 · Sharpen Your Thinking (3:40–3:52) — 12 min

### Frame (2 min)
- "Top firms — McKinsey, BCG, Google — don't test what you know. They test how you THINK."
- "Three tools: Ask, Structure, Refine"
- Show toolkit section on screen

### Exercise 1: Ask (3 min)
- Read the prompt: "I'm not good at interviews. How do I fix that?"
- "What's wrong with this question? It's too vague. Rewrite it."
- 90 sec individual writing
- 2 volunteers share → highlight: specificity opens doors

### Exercise 2: Structure (4 min)
- Briefly explain MECE: "No overlap, full coverage"
- Read prompt: "What holds people back from showing their best selves?"
- "Sort into 2–3 clean buckets on your handout"
- 2 min writing → 2 volunteers share
- Validate different bucket systems: "There's no single right answer — clean thinking IS the answer"

### Exercise 3: Refine (3 min)
- Read the "bad advice": memorize, avoid weaknesses, stick to what you know
- "What's missing? Write it down."
- 90 sec → quick popcorn share
- Land the point: "Growth > safety. Interviewers want to see how you LEARN, not just what you already know."

---

## 5 · Close & Next Steps (3:52–4:00) — 8 min

### Synthesis (3 min)
- "Today you discovered three things:"
  - Your natural instinct (the animal)
  - A structure for your stories (HIVE)
  - How to think sharper (Ask → Structure → Refine)
- "These aren't interview tricks. They're THINKING tools. They work in networking, emails, presentations — everywhere."

### BCG Visit Preview (2 min)
- "Next week — BCG site visit, February 27"
- "Bring your HIVE story. You'll have a chance to use it."
- "Think about: how does your instinct animal show up in a consulting context?"

### Closing Quote + Send-Off (3 min)
- Show closing Goodall quote on screen
- "Only when our clever brain and our human heart work together..."
- "Your instinct is your heart. Your HIVE is your brain. Together — that's your interview."
- "Take your handout. Practice your 60-second story once more this week."
- "Thank you for showing up and being brave today."

## Contingency Notes

| If... | Then... |
|-------|---------|
| Low attendance (<5) | Skip pair work, do round-robin storytelling as group |
| Someone freezes on animal | "Which prompt excites you most? Start there." |
| Time runs short | Cut Exercise 3 (Refine), keep Ask + Structure |
| Time runs long | Extend partner practice — it's the highest-value activity |
| Tech fails | Handout is self-contained — run entirely from paper |
| Energy drops | "Stand up, shake it out, sit back down" — 30 sec reset |
